<p>Videos can be powerful tools for communication in education. They can
range from lecture formats, to more informal chats and live
demonstrations. Videos are designed to be engaging and deliver content
in a more digestible way to help your learners achieve their objectives.
They can also be used to help convey complex topics in a more insightful
manner.</p>

<h2><a id="user-content-tips-for-presenters" class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" href="#tips-for-presenters"><svg class="octicon octicon-link" viewBox="0 0 16 16" version="1.1" width="16" height="16" aria-hidden="true"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>Tips for presenters</h2>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Act naturally</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Don't smile all the time</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Don't freeze all the time</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Avoid saying 'um' too much (and like, ah, uh, well etc.)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Act casual, and confident. You know your work, otherwise you
wouldn't be the one in front of the camera</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Use small gestures, some times</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Speak at your natural pace</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Speak clearly, but not monotonously</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<p>Project energy, but don't go wild</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Try not to fidget</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Are you passionate? Great, project it!</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Try not to shift in your place too much</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Enthusiasm is contagious</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<p>Strategic pausing</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Especially after important points to provide focus</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Useful after questions to help your audience pay attention</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<p>Downward inflexions</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Makes sentences not sound like questions</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Use upward inflexions only after questions</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<p>Memorise the script</p>
<ul>
<li>But don't sound like a zombie or robot</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<p>Clothing</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Try to wear plain clothes for contrast</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Smart casual is best</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Nothing that might be offensive</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Avoid green, and anything too bright</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Avoid anything distracting or too flashy (like huge jewellery)</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<p>Practice makes perfect!</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>It doesn't always happen on the first take</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>People make mistakes, and that's totally okay</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
